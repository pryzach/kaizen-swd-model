What if ?

There are a lot of methodologies/models out there; they all help you increase development speed and deliver product as soon as possible and most importantly - they work! until…. until some unit/entity isn't performing as well as we expect and everyone is waiting for them. As a result we were able to be leaders for 80% of race… but time when you cross that finish line - is actually time which counts.
What if we will focus to sustainability, direction and big picture (long term) and provide reasonably fast and steady delivery.
What if we can improve cooperation, improve atmosphere in workplace and encapsulate pride in workmanship, so employees were able to transfer that pride into product which company can be proud of.
What if along the way we would remove waste and improve process within the company.

History

We’ve heard word “revolution” so many times, but everything still stays the same… it's because systems became so big, requirements became so high and users got so used to existing features that we just cannot tore something down to build new.
As this model is based on existing ideas, the same way we need to build on existing (best) products to keep competitive; recreating doesn’t always lead to expected outcome and not many companies can do it in current highly demanding and competitive world. It is better to stand on the shoulders of the giants, than trying to create mountains in hopes to get even higher.

Challenges

We’ve heard that many times: when Business says that it is IT’s fault, and IT claims that they did what they’ve been asked to do.
We’ve seen that many times: newly hired (eager) employee comes to work to face new challenges head on... and after just a year leaves that workplace which he/she was so eager to get in only a year ago.
We’ve done that many times: we created product which is great, but with time many became unhappy with its performance/security, interface and/or its support costs / development times.

We need to improve cooperation, improve pride in workmanship, provide single direction and remove waste.

Ok, how ?

It is possible that you've heard something about Lean Manufacturing; I am not going to go into details, I will just tell that it was meant to improve process: goal is to deliver value and remove the rest (waste - muda). Along with it - Kaizen philosophy was born.
All above was created for Manufacturing process improvement, but by taking their goals and applying them to Software Development – Kaizen software development model was born.

If, after reading all of the above you are still interested – below you will find main points of the model.

Information

In current digital and fast paced world – we are constantly bombarded with information. I don’t think it is reasonable to join the trend, that’s why I am trying to point out only the most interesting/important points of the model here. If those would be enough to pique your interest – you may freely follow link in the end and read detailed description of it.

This model was build with 4 major goals: cooperation, talent, pride and waste removal:

Improve cooperation, by promoting commitment of all participants
  • No pigs and chickens: everybody is involved, committed and responsible
  • Lack of balance and responsibility for the decision - might influence morale and engagement. Everyone should be committed at the same level as we all share the same goal.
  • Every decision is made in pairs, which promotes cooperation and shared responsibility / commitment.
Promote talent and innovation while providing single direction/vision
  • Best people are selected for the job.
  • Nobody knows stakeholder requirements better than person that gathered them, as well as nobody knows technology as technical people.
  • BA provides business requirements knowledge to the team while Tech Lead/ SMEs provide technical leadership and direction.
Promotion of responsibility, involvement and pride in workmanship
  • Pairs have equal impact on the project, which promotes responsibility and involvement.
  • Shared responsibility, removal of waste and promotion of talent - improves pride in workmanship.
Providing visibility of waste and continuous improvement of process
  • Ability to see where things went wrong and improve it in the next cycle.
  • Every decision is recorded, impact is analysed and waste identified (during cycle end).
